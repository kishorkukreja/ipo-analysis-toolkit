# Overrides, Caveats, and Safety

Overrides cap the score label. They should not silently change category points. Show raw score, cap applied, and reason.

## Critical Overrides

Cap at `Weak` or `Avoid-level financial risk` when any of these are material:

- Auditor qualification affecting revenue, profit, assets, debt, going concern, or cash flow and not adjusted in restated financials.
- Negative operating cash flow in two or more years while reporting PAT profits, with no credible working-capital explanation.
- Material revenue-recognition concern or unexplained receivables spike.
- Negative net worth unless post-issue recapitalization is central and credible.
- Interest coverage below 1.0x for a debt-funded non-financial company.
- Bank/NBFC capital close to or below requirement without credible post-issue buffer.
- Bank/NBFC GNPA/NNPA sharply deteriorating with weak provision cover.
- Pharma facility import alert or warning letter affecting material revenue/capacity.
- Going-concern language or severe liquidity stress.
- Related-party transactions materially driving revenue/profit or circular funding.
- Contingent liability or litigation that could materially impair net worth.

## Serious Overrides

Cap at `Moderate` unless evidence supports a stricter cap:

- Top customer above 30% of revenue for a non-financial company.
- Top customer above 40% for an SME, unless contracted with strong payment history.
- Top 5 customers above 60% of revenue.
- Revenue CAGR above 25% while DSO or receivable days deteriorate materially.
- Large working-capital funding object in the IPO with weak CFO.
- Debt repayment improves leverage, but pre-issue leverage is high.
- PAT materially supported by one-off other income.
- ROE inflated by very low net worth or recent restructuring.
- KPI certification or formulas are unclear where KPIs drive issue-price justification.
- Restated financials were prepared by a non-statutory peer-reviewed CA and the offer document flags limitations.

## Positive Modifiers

Positive modifiers can strengthen rationale and confidence, but they do not override critical red flags:

- Consistent revenue, EBITDA, PAT, and CFO growth across three full years.
- Cumulative CFO exceeds cumulative PAT.
- Fresh-issue debt repayment materially reduces D/E and improves interest cover.
- Strong ROCE with low leverage.
- Negative working capital from durable customer advances or deferred revenue.
- Transparent, certified KPI definitions.
- Stable margins despite raw-material volatility.

## Current IPO Defaults

- Current listing timeline default is T+3 working days, where T is issue close date.
- T+3 is mandatory for public issues opening on or after 2023-12-01.
- Treat T+6 as historical unless the issue is older and source-dated.
- SME process assumptions may depend on issue opening date. For issues opening on or after 2025-07-01, verify exchange/SEBI process details before giving action-specific process guidance.

## Advice Boundary

Allowed:

- Financial score labels: `Strong`, `Moderate`, `Weak`, `Avoid-level financial risk`.
- Plain-language implications and risks.
- Missing-data caveats and confidence.
- Handoff fields for other IPO skills.

Not allowed:

- `Apply`, `Avoid`, `Subscribe`, or personalized buy/sell recommendations.
- Guaranteed returns, guaranteed allotment, or guaranteed listing gain.
- Treating GMP as official or sufficient for any decision.
- Scoring valuation or promoter-exit structure as financial quality points.

## Caveat Language

Use concise caveats:

- "This is a financial-quality score, not an application recommendation."
- "Primary financials were unavailable; confidence is Low and the score should be refreshed from the RHP/prospectus."
- "The abridged prospectus was used only to locate data; scoring numbers were cross-checked against the full offer document."
- "Valuation context was found, but valuation scoring belongs to peer comparison."
