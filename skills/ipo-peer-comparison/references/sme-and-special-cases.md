# SME and Special Cases

## SME IPO Caveat

For every SME output, include this substance:

> SME valuation caveat: peer multiples may not translate into realizable exit value because trading lots, thin float, market-maker dependency, wider spreads, and lower institutional participation can create high impact cost after listing.

SME analysis must identify the platform, issue open date, lot size, minimum application amount, and listing platform when available. If the issue opens on or after 2025-07-01 and the answer touches process mechanics, verify the open date and relevant exchange/SEBI guidance before applying process-specific SME assumptions.

## SME Valuation Adjustments

| Condition | Valuation impact |
|---|---|
| Direct SME peers exist but are illiquid | Use with caution; stale prices possible |
| Mainboard peers are much larger | Apply size/liquidity discount |
| Only one DRHP peer | Low confidence |
| Issue priced above mainboard peer median | Strong red flag |
| High ROE on tiny net worth | Check sustainability |
| PAT growth with weak OCF | Red flag |
| Related-party revenue or material RPTs | Discount required |

SME premium bands:

| Premium/discount to peer median | SME interpretation |
|---:|---|
| More than 20% discount | Possibly attractive, still check liquidity |
| -20% to 0% | Fair/acceptable |
| 0% to +15% | Needs strong justification |
| +15% to +30% | Expensive |
| More than +30% | Avoid/overvalued unless exceptional |

## Loss-Making and New-Age Issuers

Do not compute fake P/E. Required language:

- `P/E: Not meaningful because PAT/EPS is negative`
- `RoNW: Negative or not meaningful`
- Primary valuation based on EV/Sales, P/S, gross profit multiple, ARR, AUM, GMV/take-rate economics, or sector KPI.
- KPI trend table where disclosed.
- Past transaction/WACA comparison when disclosed.
- Explicit path-to-profitability assessment.

KPI examples:

| Business | KPIs |
|---|---|
| SaaS | ARR, NRR, churn, CAC payback, gross margin, EBITDA margin, rule of 40 |
| Marketplace | GMV, take rate, contribution margin, monthly transacting users, order frequency |
| Payments | TPV/GMV, take rate, MDR, active merchants, contribution profit |
| Lending fintech | AUM, disbursements, GNPA, credit cost, collection efficiency, ROA, co-lending mix |
| Consumer internet | MAU/DAU, paid users, repeat rate, AOV, contribution margin |
| D2C | Repeat purchase, gross margin, CAC, channel mix |

Loss-making verdict logic:

- High growth plus improving gross margin, contribution margin, and burn: premium may be considered.
- High growth but negative contribution margin: speculative and low confidence.
- Slowing growth and widening losses: avoid/overvalued unless priced at a steep discount.
- IPO price materially above recent WACA without KPI improvement: red flag.

## Past Transaction Pricing

For issuers with KPI and WACA disclosures, extract:

- Primary issuance WACA in prior 18 months.
- Secondary transaction WACA in prior 18 months.
- IPO floor/cap price as a multiple of WACA.
- Management explanation for uplift.

Interpretation:

- IPO below recent institutional round can indicate public-market discount, but check deterioration risk.
- IPO 2x-5x recent WACA needs strong KPI improvement.
- IPO above private valuation while growth/margins deteriorated is a red flag.

## Other Special Cases

Negative EBITDA:

- EV/EBITDA is not meaningful.
- Use EV/Sales, gross profit multiple, or KPI multiple.
- Explain whether losses are scale investment or weak economics.

One-off profit:

- Compute adjusted P/E excluding exceptional income where data is available.
- Show both reported and adjusted P/E.

Cyclicals:

- Avoid declaring cheapness from peak-cycle P/E.
- Use mid-cycle EBITDA/PAT and margin context where possible.

Recently profitable:

- Use latest P/E with low-history caveat.
- Add P/S or EV/Sales.

Conglomerate or multi-segment issuer:

- Use segment-level peer comparisons or sum-of-the-parts if data supports it.
- Otherwise mark peer comparison low confidence.

Regulated businesses:

- Include regulatory risk.
- Use sector-specific KPIs and not just P/E.
