# Workflows And Troubleshooting

## Broker Or Intermediary UPI ASBA

1. Confirm issue type, category, dates, price band or fixed price, lot size, and registrar.
2. Confirm the applicant has active demat details, correct PAN, sufficient clear bank balance, and their own UPI ID linked to their own bank account.
3. In the broker/intermediary app, open IPO, Bids, Public Issues, or Apply IPO.
4. Select the issue, category, lots/quantity, and price or cut-off if available.
5. Submit the bid and note application/order number.
6. Open the UPI app and approve the IPO mandate after checking issue name, amount, and bank account.
7. Verify bank lien/block status. Broker status can lag.
8. After close, check registrar first, exchange alternatives second, broker app third.

UPI mandate acceptance generally runs until 5:00 PM on IPO closing day. For revised SME process issues, final-day bidding closes at 4:00 PM and UPI confirmation remains available until 5:00 PM.

## Bank ASBA

1. Log into the applicant's SCSB netbanking or mobile banking.
2. Open IPO, ASBA, Public Issue, or Investments section.
3. Select the issue.
4. Enter investor category, PAN, demat DP ID/client ID, lots/quantity, and price/cut-off if available.
5. Confirm sufficient clear balance and submit.
6. Save the application/reference number.
7. Check the account lien/block amount.
8. Track allotment using registrar or exchange status tools.

Prefer bank ASBA for applications above INR 5 lakh, bNII applications, repeated UPI mandate failures, unsupported broker routes/categories, or users who want direct bank blocking.

## Revision And Cancellation

Before advising, ask:

- Is the issue mainboard or SME?
- What is the issue open date?
- Which category did the user bid under?
- Has the bid already been submitted?
- Is the issue still open?
- Was the UPI mandate already approved or the bank ASBA lien created?

Mainboard retail users can generally revise or withdraw while the issue is open, subject to exchange, broker, and bank operating windows. If a mandate is already approved, block release may take time.

Mainboard NII/HNI and QIB users cannot withdraw or lower bid size. Upward revision may be possible depending on platform and mandate handling.

For SME IPOs under revised July 2025 process, cancellation and downward modification are unavailable to every category.

## Allotment Check

1. Identify registrar from RHP/prospectus, exchange page, or official IPO notice.
2. Use registrar allotment page when active.
3. Enter only the requested identifier: PAN, application number, or DP/client ID.
4. If registrar is unavailable, use BSE application/allotment status or NSE bid verification where applicable.
5. Treat broker app as convenience display, not the primary source.

Common registrars: KFin Technologies, MUFG Intime/Link Intime, Bigshare Services, and Maashitla Securities.

## Troubleshooting

### No Mandate Received

- Confirm bid was submitted before broker/exchange cutoff.
- Check whether UPI ID is valid, belongs to the applicant, and is supported for IPO mandates.
- Wait for routing lag, then check broker order status.
- If the issue is still open, consider cancelling/reapplying only if category rules permit it. Avoid duplicate same-PAN applications without clear cancellation confirmation.
- If time is short or UPI repeatedly fails, use bank ASBA where available.

### Mandate Approved, Broker Pending

- Check the UPI app mandate history and bank lien/block status.
- Save the application number and mandate reference.
- Allow for sponsor bank, NPCI, exchange, and broker status sync lag.
- Escalate to broker, bank, or exchange verification if the issue is near close.

### Amount Blocked But Not Allotted

- Explain that ASBA release is unblock/lien release.
- Check registrar status and bank account lien status.
- SCSB unblock can lag after basis of allotment finalization; avoid giving an exact release guarantee.
- Escalate to bank/SCSB first for lien release, then registrar if allotment status is inconsistent.

### Allotted But Shares Not In Demat

- Check the issue's tentative T+2 demat credit schedule and holidays.
- Confirm demat account is active and DP ID/client ID in the application was correct.
- Contact registrar and DP if shares are allotted but not credited before listing.

## Common Mistakes

- UPI mandate not approved before cutoff.
- Wrong or third-party UPI ID.
- Third-party bank account.
- PAN, bank, UPI, or demat mismatch.
- Insufficient clear balance.
- Multiple applications under same PAN for the same IPO/category.
- Quantity not in lot multiples.
- Bid price outside price band.
- Mainboard retail bid below final issue price because cut-off was not selected.
- Applying as retail when cap-price amount exceeds INR 2 lakh.
- Trying to cancel or reduce HNI/NII or revised-process SME bids after submission.
- Waiting until the last hour and missing broker, bank, exchange, or mandate cutoff.
- Wrong DP ID/client ID in bank ASBA.
- Inactive demat account.
