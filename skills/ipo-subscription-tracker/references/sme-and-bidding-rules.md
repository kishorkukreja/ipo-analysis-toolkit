# SME and Bidding Rules Reference

## Required SME Fields

For every SME IPO, capture:

- Platform: NSE SME/Emerge or BSE SME.
- Issue open date and close date.
- Lot size and minimum application amount.
- Market maker or liquidity context when disclosed.
- Whether the issue is before or after the 2025-07-01 SME process change.

## SME Interpretation

SME IPO subscription is not directly comparable to mainboard subscription. Add caveats for:

- Smaller issue size and free float.
- Larger minimum application value.
- Lower post-listing liquidity and wider spreads.
- Market maker mechanics.
- Lower analyst coverage.
- Greater risk that GMP and subscription are influenced by a small set of participants.

Use conservative wording:

```text
This is an SME IPO. High subscription and GMP are less reliable than in mainboard IPOs because float is smaller, liquidity is thinner, and grey-market signals are easier to influence.
```

Do not call SME retail or NII demand "excellent" without mentioning liquidity and small-float risk.

## July 2025 SME Process Handling

For SME IPOs opening on or after 2025-07-01:

1. Verify the issue open date.
2. Verify current exchange/SEBI or issue-document rules before giving process-specific guidance.
3. Check minimum application size, lot rules, cut-off bidding availability, bid revision and cancellation windows, final-day timing, and platform-specific instructions.
4. If official confirmation is missing, say the process detail is unverified and avoid exact application instructions.

For SME IPOs opening before 2025-07-01, state that older SME process assumptions may apply, subject to exchange-specific transition guidance.

## Cutoff vs Price Bidding

Mainboard book-built retail:

- Cutoff bidding means the investor agrees to pay the final discovered issue price.
- It reduces the risk of a valid retail bid being below the final issue price.
- It does not improve lottery odds once the retail application is valid.

Specific price bidding:

- A bid below the final issue price may not be considered.
- A bid at the upper price band can be similar to cutoff for cash blocking but is not always the same instruction in the application system.

Do not recommend cutoff bidding when:

- The issue is fixed-price.
- The category or platform does not offer cutoff bidding.
- The IPO is an SME issue and post-2025-07-01 mechanics have not been verified from official sources.

## Timeline

Use T+3 working days as the current default, where T is the issue close date. T+3 is mandatory for public issues opening on or after 2023-12-01. Treat T+6 as historical unless a historical issue or official document proves it applies.
