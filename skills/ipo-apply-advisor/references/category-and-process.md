# Category And Process Rules

## Timeline

Use T+3 working days as the current Indian IPO listing timeline baseline. T is the issue closing date. T+3 is mandatory for public issues opening on or after 2023-12-01.

Do not describe T+6 as the current default. For historical IPOs, verify the applicable issue timetable before commenting.

## Mainboard Retail

Recommend `Retail` when:

- Application amount is up to Rs.2 lakh.
- User is a normal retail investor.
- IPO is or may be oversubscribed.
- User is not trying to make duplicate applications across accounts/PANs.

Guidance:

- For book-built mainboard retail applications, bid at cut-off only if upper-band valuation remains acceptable.
- Applying for multiple lots in oversubscribed retail usually blocks more funds without meaningfully improving allotment odds.
- One valid application per eligible PAN is the practical allotment unit in oversubscribed retail.

## Mainboard sHNI

Recommend `sHNI` only when:

- User can comfortably block more than Rs.2 lakh and up to Rs.10 lakh.
- Listing-gain and/or long-term score is strong.
- sHNI subscription is not already extreme.
- User understands application-wise allotment uncertainty.

Do not recommend sHNI when verdict is Neutral/Avoid, GMP is the only positive, liquidity is needed during the IPO window, or payment route feasibility is unclear.

## Mainboard bHNI

Recommend `bHNI` only when the user has high conviction, can block more than Rs.10 lakh, QIB/anchor quality is strong, valuation is not stretched, and downside risk is understood.

Avoid bHNI recommendations when NII subscription is already extreme, listing gain is the only thesis, market is correcting, or the issue is SME with material liquidity risk.

## SME Individual Logic

Always identify platform: NSE Emerge or BSE SME. SME analysis must mention liquidity risk, larger lot-value friction, market maker context, spread/depth risk, and exit difficulty.

For SME IPOs opening on or after 2025-07-01, verify the current prospectus and exchange process. Current exchange references indicate revised SME individual applications generally require at least two lots and application size above Rs.2 lakh, and cut-off bidding may not be available in the same way as mainboard retail.

SME defaults:

| SME setup | Category guidance |
|---|---|
| Strong fundamentals, fair valuation, clean governance, real demand | Apply only if user accepts lot-size and exit risk |
| High GMP but weak financials | Avoid or Neutral; do not chase |
| Weak cash conversion or RPT concerns | Avoid |
| Unclear category/payment details | Ask for broker/exchange route or prospectus details |

Market maker presence is not bullish by itself. Treat it as minimum liquidity support, not proof of easy exit.

## Cut-Off Price

When amount is needed, calculate `lot size x bid price x number of lots`. For a book-built retail IPO at cut-off, use the upper price band as the bid price. Example: 42 shares at Rs.340 for one lot means Rs.14,280.

For book-built mainboard retail applications, default to:

`Yes - bid at cut-off because issue is likely to price at upper band and the apply thesis survives upper-band valuation.`

Use `No` or a specific-price bid when:

- User wants to avoid allotment above fair value.
- Upper band looks expensive.
- Price band is wide enough to change the thesis.
- Non-retail or SME category rules require price-specific bidding.

## ASBA And UPI

Flag payment feasibility. UPI IPO flows have transaction limits and broker/bank-specific behavior; larger applications may require bank ASBA or other permitted routes. Do not assume a user's broker/payment route supports the recommended category.
