# Timeline And SME Reference

## Current T+3 Timeline

Use T+3 as the current Indian public issue timeline unless the issue is historical or an official source says otherwise. T is the issue closing date. Working days exclude Sundays, exchange holidays, and applicable bank holidays.

| Stage | Current target |
|---|---|
| Issue closes | T |
| UPI mandate acceptance | T by 5 pm |
| Final rejection and basis of allotment work | T+1 |
| Stock exchange approval of basis | T+1 by evening |
| Debit/unblock instructions initiated | T+2 morning |
| Fund transfer for allottees | T+2 by afternoon |
| Unblocking for non-allottees | T+2 by late afternoon |
| Demat credit corporate action | T+2, normally by evening |
| Allotment advertisement on issuer/BRLM/RTA sites | T+2 by night |
| Listing/trading starts | T+3 |
| Newspaper allotment advertisement | T+3, not later than T+4 |

If the user complains before T+2/T+3, classify the case as likely still within the normal window. If non-allotment and funds remain blocked after the normal window, provide bank/broker/registrar escalation.

## Refund, UPI Mandate, And ASBA

IPO application money is blocked under ASBA/UPI and debited only for allotted shares. Non-allottees should see the mandate released or the lien unblocked. A bank balance may not change visibly if the issue was only a lien/hold; ask the user to check lien or hold details as well as transaction history.

Common interpretations:

- Mandate released and no debit: usually non-allotment or invalid/not processed application; confirm with registrar/broker.
- Amount debited: likely allotted or partially allotted; confirm allotted shares and demat credit.
- Amount still blocked after non-allotment: bank/sponsor-bank unblock delay; escalate if outside normal T+3 working-day window.
- Mandate expired without debit: likely non-allotment or application failure; verify the application status.

## Demat Credit

If allotted, shares should normally be credited by T+2 evening or before listing, subject to holidays and processing delays. Ask the user to check broker holdings, CDSL/NSDL alerts, and depository emails/SMS. If registrar says allotted but demat credit is missing after the expected window, contact broker/DP and registrar with application number and DP/client ID.

## SME IPO Handling

Always identify whether the IPO is mainboard, NSE Emerge, or BSE SME.

SME allotment checking mechanics are similar, but expectations differ:

- Minimum ticket size and lot-size risk are usually higher.
- Post-listing liquidity and spreads are usually riskier.
- Smaller registrar sites may be slower, issue-specific, or less polished.
- Basis of allotment PDFs may be easier to find through exchange announcements than registrar dropdowns.

For SME IPOs opening on or after 2025-07-01:

- Use "Individual Investor" rather than old retail framing when the issue documents do.
- Minimum bid size is 2 lots with application size above Rs 2 lakh.
- Cut-off price bidding is not available.
- Downward modification and cancellation are not available.
- Bidding closes at 4 pm on the last day; UPI confirmation remains up to 5 pm.

Do not apply post-2025 SME process details unless the issue opening date supports them.
