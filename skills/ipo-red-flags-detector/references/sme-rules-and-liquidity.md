# SME Rules and Liquidity Reference

Use this for NSE Emerge and BSE SME IPOs. Always state the issue-open date and platform. If the open date is unknown, do not make process-specific conclusions; ask for or verify the date.

## Date Handling

- Current repository default IPO timeline is T+3.
- Treat T+6 as historical unless the issue is older or official documents specify it.
- For SME IPOs opening on or after 2025-07-01, verify and apply the revised SME process/rule assumptions from current SEBI and exchange sources.
- Do not apply post-2025-07-01 SME process-specific guidance when the issue-open date is unknown.

## Researched SME Framework Points

Verify current sources before final regulatory conclusions. The research set captured these NSE/SEBI points:

| Area | Detector treatment |
|---|---|
| Operating profit | Check whether operating profit from operations satisfies current SME eligibility thresholds. |
| Net worth and FCFE | Check positive net worth and positive FCFE patterns where platform criteria require them. |
| OFS cap | Treat SME OFS above current platform cap as regulatory/eligibility risk. Research noted NSE Emerge OFS by selling shareholders capped at 20% of issue size. |
| Selling shareholder limit | Check whether selling shareholders exceed current holding-sale limits. |
| Promoter/related-party loan repayment | Treat direct or indirect repayment from issue proceeds as a hard red flag where current SME platform rules prohibit it. |
| GCP | Research noted SEBI approval of an SME GCP cap of 15% of amount raised or Rs. 10 crore, whichever is lower. Verify current status. |
| Monitoring agency | Check whether fresh issue size triggers monitoring agency requirements under current rules. |
| RPT materiality | Research noted SME listed-entity RPT threshold of 10% consolidated turnover or Rs. 50 crore, whichever is lower. This is a governance context, not a complete investor-risk threshold. |

## SME Risk Multiplier

Apply one severity uplift or +25% score for accounting, governance, and liquidity flags because SME IPOs often have thinner liquidity, higher lot-size friction, less institutional coverage, and higher dependence on gatekeepers.

Auto-reject or Critical composite patterns:

1. Sudden revenue/PAT growth with negative OCF or receivables spike.
2. RPT-backed revenue, purchases, loans, or customer concentration.
3. GCP near cap, working-capital-heavy objects, and weak monitoring.
4. Promoter OFS, pledge, default history, or pre-IPO transfer pattern.
5. Small/opaque auditor, weak merchant banker, and unclear market-maker depth.
6. Extreme subscription or GMP hype unsupported by cash flow and governance evidence.
7. Promoter or group-company history of bonus/split/preferential allotment followed by offload.

## Market Maker and Liquidity Scan

For every SME IPO, report:

- Market maker name(s), if disclosed.
- Merchant banker/lead manager and underwriting role.
- Free float and issue size.
- Lot size and minimum application amount.
- Whether market-making obligations are clearly described.
- That market making reduces but does not eliminate liquidity risk.
- That obligations can be time-limited and bid-ask spreads can still be wide.

Flag as Major/Critical when the market maker is missing, weakly disclosed, apparently inexperienced, conflicted without disclosure, or when the free float is tiny relative to expected retail exit demand.
