# Retail Interpretation Guide

## Framing

This skill gives document-grounded research support. It does not give personalized investment advice and does not guarantee allotment, listing gains, returns, or the accuracy of informal market signals.

Separate every major finding into:

- Fact: what the document or official source says.
- Interpretation: why it matters to a retail investor.
- Missing data: what could not be verified.
- Next step: another IPO skill or source to check.

## Business Snapshot

Summarize:

- What the company sells or provides.
- Main revenue streams and customer segments.
- Geography and industry exposure.
- Claimed moat or differentiator, clearly labeled as issuer claim if not independently verified.
- Major dependencies such as top customers, licenses, suppliers, facilities, or regulations.

## Offer Structure

Use plain interpretation:

- Fresh issue: money goes to the company after issue expenses.
- OFS: money generally goes to selling shareholders, not the company.
- High OFS does not automatically mean avoid, but it changes the question from "growth funding" to "exit and valuation quality."
- Promoter OFS needs extra governance context: remaining stake, reason for sale, related-party transactions, and litigation.

## Financial Snapshot

Keep this lighter than `ipo-financials-scorer`:

- Show three fiscal years where available.
- Include revenue, PAT, operating cash flow, debt, and margins if safely extractable.
- Flag negative operating cash flow, declining PAT, high debt, deteriorating margins, and heavy working capital.
- Do not produce a full financial score unless the user asks to hand off to `ipo-financials-scorer`.

## Litigation and Regulatory Proceedings

Classify by:

- Issuer/company.
- Subsidiary.
- Promoter/promoter group.
- Directors/KMP.
- Group companies.
- Tax, criminal, civil, regulatory, securities, labor, environmental, or other.

Retail meaning should state whether the issue is:

- Financially material.
- Governance-sensitive.
- License or business-continuity sensitive.
- Routine but still disclosed.
- Unclear because amount/status is missing.

## SME vs Mainboard

For mainboard IPOs, emphasize institutional demand, peer valuation, retail allotment odds, and standard category structure only when current sources support it.

For SME IPOs, add:

- Higher liquidity and bid-ask spread risk.
- Larger minimum application amount and lot sensitivity.
- Market maker context.
- Platform distinction: NSE Emerge or BSE SME.
- Conservative treatment of GMP and oversubscription.
- For issues opening on or after 2025-07-01, do not assume cut-off bidding or old retail category mechanics.

## Safety Language

Use:

- "This is a document-risk summary, not a recommendation."
- "The finding increases/decreases risk because..."
- "Run a current GMP/subscription/peer-comparison check before an apply decision."

Avoid:

- "Guaranteed listing gain."
- "Sure-shot allotment."
- "Apply with confidence" without context.
- Treating GMP or oversubscription as official proof of returns.
