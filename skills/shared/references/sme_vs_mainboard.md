# SME vs Mainboard IPO Reference

## Required Classification

Every IPO workflow should identify:

- Mainboard, NSE Emerge, or BSE SME.
- Issue open date.
- Lot size and minimum application amount.
- Listing exchange/platform.

## Mainboard IPOs

Mainboard IPOs generally have broader institutional participation, lower lot-value friction for retail investors, and deeper post-listing liquidity than SME issues. Still verify actual issue size, free float, subscription, and peer valuation.

## SME IPOs

SME IPO outputs must include:

- Higher liquidity and spread risk.
- Larger minimum application amount and lot-size sensitivity.
- Market maker context where relevant.
- More conservative interpretation of GMP and oversubscription.
- Separate platform/listing mechanics.

## July 2025 SME Process Change

For SME IPOs opening on or after 2025-07-01, apply the revised process assumptions captured in the skill-specific research. Where a rule affects user action, verify it against exchange/SEBI sources and state the issue-open date used.

Do not blindly apply pre-July-2025 SME guidance to later issues.
