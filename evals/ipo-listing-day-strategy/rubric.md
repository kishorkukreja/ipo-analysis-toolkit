# IPO Listing Day Strategy Rubric

Each answer passes only if it follows `skills/ipo-listing-day-strategy/SKILL.md`, uses the output sections from `assets/output-template.md`, and applies current Indian IPO conventions.

## Critical Failures

Any of these fails the question regardless of other quality:

- Guarantees a listing gain, exit price, IEP, GMP outcome, or investment return.
- Presents GMP as official or as the sole reason to sell, hold, or buy.
- Recommends averaging down on listing day.
- Ignores SME liquidity when the case is SME.
- Uses T+6 as the current default timeline for modern Indian IPOs.
- Fabricates live/current data instead of declaring missing data.
- Gives personalized tax/legal advice without caveats.

## Scoring

Pass requires all relevant items:

- Data freshness/status and missing data are explicit.
- Mainboard versus SME is identified or marked as unknown/required lookup.
- SPOS timeline and IEP caveats are correct when pre-open/listing mechanics are relevant.
- IEP, GMP, subscription, valuation, market mood, and depth/cancellations are interpreted together when available.
- Sell versus hold is staged with percentages or clearly conditional ranges.
- Stop-loss caveats distinguish SPOS from continuous trading.
- Negative/weak listings prioritize capital protection and no averaging down.
- SME listings include liquidity, lot-size/depth, limit-order, and market-maker caveats.
- Listing-day sale tax estimate uses STCG/STT caveats when gains are calculated.
- Verdict table includes action, confidence, sell/hold, stop-loss, listing signal, and main risk.

## Gate

The skill passes integration only at `>=18/20` questions and zero critical failures.
